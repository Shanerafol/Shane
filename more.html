Mother Tongue

Mother tongue, as defined by the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA), is the language or dialect spoken at home or at birth of the child, or the language of the child if only he had the power of speech that early. It may also refer to the language or dialect spoken most of the time by members of the household.

Cebuano is still the most common and predominant language/dialect used in households. However, certain ethnic groups speak their respective dialects and/or languages, such as Subano, Waray, Hiligaynon and Ilocano.

Ethnic Origin

The population of Mahayag is divided into various ethnic groups namely: Cebuano, Subanen, Muslim, Kalibugan. The majority of the people living in the municipality are Cebuano (50%), followed by Subanen (30%), Kalibugan (15%), and Muslim (5%).

Household of Indigenous People

Most of the barangays in the municipality have residents who are recognized as members of Indigenous People, commonly known as IPs. Although the majority of the residents belong to the Subanen group (50%), some are also considered Kalibugan (25%).

Religious Affiliation

The recent available data on religious affiliation is based on the 2014 Rapid Community Based Monitoring System (RCBMS) facilitated by the National Anti-Poverty Commission (NAPC). Roman Catholic is the dominant religion (77.98%), followed by Seventh Day Adventist (SDA) (4.13%), and the Assembly of God (2.16%).

Festival

Festivals in the Municipality, locally known as fiestas, originated during the Spanish colonial period. Each barangay has a patron saint, and fiestas are held to honor them or commemorate history and culture. These events may include Holy Masses, processions, parades, theatrical plays, reenactments, religious or cultural rituals, trade fairs, exhibits, concerts, pageants, and various games and contests.
